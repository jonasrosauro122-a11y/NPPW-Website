Detailed flow by lane

Each lane shows how human support and automation work together.

Acuity → Jobber

1. Intake received

Acuity captures store, service type, date, and contact info.

2. VA checks fields

Phone, address, and booking integrity are validated.

3. Zapier routes data

Clean data is mapped into Jobber fields.

4. Jobber updates

Scheduling and invoicing-prep visibility is created.

ServiceChannel

1. Open work order

Confirm site, store number, service date, and vendor.

2. Check proof

Review check-in / check-out, time on site, and photos.

3. Apply rules

Compare duration and photos to compliance expectations.

4. Decide outcome

Mark verified, needs clarification, or escalate.

Exception Queue

1. Issue detected

Missing data, missing proof, or suspicious duration.

2. Flow pauses

Bad data is blocked from continuing downstream.

3. Notify & document

VA logs the issue and follows escalation script.

4. Resume or escalate

After correction, continue or escalate to specialist lead.

Brand & Growth Support

1. Audit

Review service descriptions and online listings.

2. Update

Improve GBP, local content, and service consistency.

3. Track changes

Maintain update log and approval needs.

4. Report impact

Show completed updates and visibility opportunities.

System ecosystem awareness

This is one of the most important visual training areas because the VA must understand how platforms connect.

System 1

ServiceChannel

Operations verification environment. Used for ticket management, proof review, and compliance status.

System 2

Jobber

Operations execution layer for scheduling visibility and invoicing preparation.

System 3

Acuity

Intake / booking source. Useful, but requires awareness of incomplete data or booking limitations.

System 4

Internal Tools

Google Sheets or internal dashboards are used for issue tracking, escalation logs, and leadership visibility.

Why this matters

The VA must know which platform starts the work, which platform manages operations, which platform verifies proof, and which platform reports the final result. This avoids duplicate work and prevents system confusion.
