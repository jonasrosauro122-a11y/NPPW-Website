ServiceChannel verification: where the VA creates the most value

This is the judgment-heavy desk function that should stay human-led. The VA becomes the quality control checkpoint between subcontractor activity and billing confidence.

Verification desk model

Verification responsibilities

1
Confirm check-in/check-outVerify presence and timing against expected duration for the service type and location pattern.
2
Validate time on siteCatch suspicious short visits, especially for recurring parking lot sweeping or multi-step service visits.
3
Review photos and attachmentsConfirm before/after or required evidence exists and is usable for client accountability.
4
Document exceptionsEscalate missing check-ins, insufficient duration, or missing proof so corrections happen before invoicing or disputes.
5
Protect billing readinessKeep tickets clean enough that specialists spend less time chasing missing information or calling for corrections.

Recommended VA queue setup

  • Queue 1: same-day verification for high-volume recurring work
  • Queue 2: exception handling for missing check-ins or missing photos
  • Queue 3: callback / ServiceChannel contact tasks
  • Queue 4: daily leadership summary with unresolved risks
Best starting model: 1 VA = verification + exception log + ticket follow-up

Standard operating process for the VA

The goal is not just to use tools. The goal is to teach the VA what “good work” looks like at each checkpoint.

Process ownership

Delivery / scheduling SOP

1
Review Acuity bookingConfirm service type, region, date, client name, phone, and address completeness.
2
Let Zapier validateAutomation checks field presence and routes clean bookings to the correct next action.
3
Confirm Jobber creationReview whether the correct record was created and whether scheduling details are usable by dispatch.
4
Log to Google SheetsAudit entry captures status, ownership, and exception notes for future follow-up.
5
Resolve exceptionsOnly touch exceptions manually so the VA focuses on edge cases, not every routine entry.

Verification SOP

1
Open ServiceChannel queueWork by priority, aging, and recurring-service importance.
2
Check proof requirementsCheck-ins, check-outs, timestamps, photos, and notes must match service expectations.
3
Apply duration logicFlag visits that are materially shorter than what that service normally requires.
4
Update exception logUse standard notes so specialists and leadership can act quickly without re-reading the ticket from scratch.
5
Close the loopDocument outcome, who owns the next step, and when the ticket should be rechecked.
